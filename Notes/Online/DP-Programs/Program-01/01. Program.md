# 🧩 Problem Title: Climbing Steps with Forbidden Tiles

---

## 🔖 Concepts Involved

- Dynamic Programming, Recursion, Memoization, State Definition

---

## 📖 Problem Story / Context

- You are climbing a staircase to reach the top.

- The staircase has **N steps**.
- You can climb either:
- 1 step at a time
- 2 steps at a time

- However…

- Some steps are **broken** and cannot be stepped on.

- Your goal is to find:

- 👉 **How many distinct ways can you reach the top (step N)** - without stepping on any broken step?

---

## 📥 Inputs

- Integer `N` → total number of steps
- Array `broken[]` → list of broken step indices

---

## 📤 Output

- Integer → number of valid ways to reach step `N`

---

## ⚙️ Constraints

- $1 \le N \le 10^5$
- $0 \le broken.length \le N$
- Steps are **1-indexed**
- You cannot land on a broken step

---

## 🧠 Edge-Case Thinking Prompts

- What if step `1` is broken?
- What if step `N` is broken?
- What if all paths are blocked?
- What if there are no broken steps?
- What if only one path exists?

---

## 🔍 Sample Input & Output

### Sample 1

- **Input:**
> ```text
> N = 5
> broken = [3]
> ```

- **Output:**
> ```text
> 2
> ```

### Explanation:

- Valid paths:
- 1 → 2 → 4 → 5
- 1 → 2 → 3 (invalid ❌ broken)

- So only 2 valid ways.

---

### Sample 2

- **Input:**
> ```text
> N = 4
> broken = []
> ```

- **Output:**
> ```text
> 5
> ```

---

## 🧠 Thinking Like a Programmer

**What must be tracked?**

- Number of ways to reach each step

**What can change?**

- Current step position

**What must never change?**

- You cannot land on broken steps

**What brute force would do?**

- Try all possible paths (exponential)

**Why is brute force bad?**

- Recomputes same subproblems repeatedly
