# 🟢 LEVEL 4 — Algorithmic Thinking Basics

- Before learning complex algorithms, students must learn **how to think about solving problems step by step**.

- An algorithm is simply:

> **A clear list of steps to solve a problem.**

- Computers cannot guess or improvise.
- They only follow **exact instructions**.

- So algorithmic thinking means:

```text
Breaking a problem into simple steps a computer can follow.
```

---

## 1️⃣ What is an Algorithm?

- An **algorithm** is a **recipe for solving a problem**.

- **Example problem:**

> Make a sandwich.

- **Steps:**

- **1. Take bread:**
- **2. Add butter:**
- **3. Add vegetables:**
- **4. Close sandwich:**

- These instructions form an **algorithm**.

- In programming we do the same thing.

---

## Example Algorithm (Find Maximum Number)

- Given numbers:

```text
3, 7, 2, 9
```

- **Goal: Find the largest number.**

- **Algorithm:**

- **1. Start with first number as max:**
- **2. Compare with next number:**
- **3. If bigger → update max:**
- **4. Repeat until end:**

- **Result:**

```text
9
```

---

## 2️⃣ Step-by-Step Problem Solving

- Computers solve problems by breaking them into **small steps**.

- **Example problem:**

> Count apples in a basket.

- **Basket:**

```text
🍎 🍎 🍎 🍎
```

- **Steps:**

- **count = 0:**
- **look at each apple:**
- **increase count:**

- **Algorithm:**

```text
count = 0
for each apple:
    count = count + 1
```

- ****

```text
count = 4
```

---

## 3️⃣ Brute Force Thinking

- The simplest way to solve a problem is often called **brute force**.

- **Brute force means:**

> **Try every possible option until you find the answer.**

---

## Example

- Find number **7** in this list:

```text
3 9 2 7 5
```

- **Brute force search:**

- **check 3:**
- **check 9:**
- **check 2:**
- **check 7  ← found:**

- This is called **linear search**.

- It works, but it may be slow.

---

## 4️⃣ Why Efficiency Matters

- Imagine searching in:

```text
1 million numbers
```

- Brute force may take **1 million checks**.

- A smarter algorithm may take only **20 checks**.

- So algorithmic thinking asks:

- **Can we solve this faster?:**

---

## 5️⃣ Repeated Work is Bad

- Suppose you solve the same math problem many times.

- **Example:**

```text
5 + 5
```

- If you recompute it every time, you waste effort.

- **Better idea:**

- **remember the answer:**

- This concept becomes important later in **Dynamic Programming**.

---

## 6️⃣ Breaking Big Problems into Small Problems

- A powerful thinking method is:

- **Big problem → smaller problems:**

- **Example:**

- Sum numbers from 1 to 5.

- Instead of adding everything at once:

```text
1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5
```

- **Think like this:**

- $sum(5) = 5 + sum(4)$
- $sum(4) = 4 + sum(3)$
- $sum(3) = 3 + sum(2)$

- We reduce the problem gradually.

- This idea leads to **recursion and DP** later.



---

## 7️⃣ Pattern Recognition

- Good algorithms often recognize **patterns**.

- **Example sequence:**

```text
2, 4, 6, 8
```

- **Pattern:**

- **+2 each time:**

- **Another example:**

```text
1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8
```

- **Pattern:**

- **next = previous two numbers added:**

- This pattern leads to **Fibonacci**, a classic DP example.

---

## 8️⃣ Input → Process → Output

- Every algorithm follows this structure.

- **Example:**

- **Input → numbers:**
- **Process → compute sum:**
- **Output → result:**

- **Example program:**

- **Input:**

```text
3 4
```

- **Process:**

- **add numbers:**

- **Output:**

```text
7
```

- This model helps organize thinking.

---

## 9️⃣ Avoiding Mistakes with Clear Steps

- Computers follow instructions **exactly**.

- If instructions are unclear, errors happen.

- **Example bad algorithm:**

- **Find the biggest number.:**

- Too vague.

- **Better algorithm:**

- **1. Assume first number is max:**
- **2. Compare with next number:**
- **3. Replace max if needed:**
- **4. Repeat until end:**

- Now the steps are precise.

---

## 🔟 Algorithm Mental Model

- When facing any problem, ask:

- **1. What is the input?:**
- **2. What is the output?:**
- **3. What steps convert input to output?:**
- **4. Can we avoid repeated work?:**

- This thinking builds the foundation for **all algorithms**.

---

## 🧠 Why This Level Matters for DP

- Dynamic Programming problems require:

- **break problem into smaller parts:**
- **reuse solutions:**
- **avoid recomputation:**

- These ideas come directly from **algorithmic thinking**.

- Without this mindset, DP feels confusing.
