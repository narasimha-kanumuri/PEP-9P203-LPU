## 📌 Problem Statement

- You are given an N × M grid representing heights of cells.

- After rainfall, water may get trapped in low-height regions.

- However, unlike the standard problem, water can flow **only in diagonal directions**:
- down-left (↙)
- down-right (↘)

- Water **cannot move** up, left, right, or straight down.

---

## 💧 Water Flow Rules

- Water can flow only through:
- (i+1, j-1)
- (i+1, j+1)

- Water can **escape** if it reaches any boundary cell.

- A cell can trap water **only if all possible diagonal paths from it are blocked by higher or equal heights**.

---

## 🎯 Objective

- Compute the **total amount of water trapped** across the grid.

---

## 📥 Input Format

- First line: two integers N and M
- Next N lines: M integers each (heights)

---

## 📤 Output Format

- A single integer → total trapped water

---

## 🔍 Example

### Input

> ```text
> 3 3
> 3 1 3
> 2 1 2
> 3 2 3
> ```

### Output

> ```text
> 2
> ```

---

## 🧠 Constraints

- 1 ≤ N, M ≤ 1000
- 0 ≤ height ≤ $10^{5}$

---

## ⚠️ Notes

- This is NOT standard 2D rainwater trapping.
- Movement is restricted → forms a **directed diagonal graph**.
- Each cell’s trapped water depends only on **reachable diagonal boundary constraints**.

---

## 🧪 Edge Cases

- Single row → no trapping
- Single column → no trapping
- Strictly increasing diagonals → no trapping
- Flat grid → no trapping

---

## 🧭 Hint (for students)

- Think in reverse:
- Instead of filling water,
- Track how water can **escape to boundary via diagonals**.

- Cells that cannot escape → trap water.
